What is PostGIS?

PostGIS extends the capabilities of the PostgreSQL relational database by adding support storing, indexing and querying geographic data.

Illustration of PostGIS For Newbies | by Elizabeth Christensen

The combination of PostgreSQL and PostGIS is widely used in applications that require spatial analysis, geographic information systems (GIS), mapping, and location-based services. It allows developers to build applications that integrate spatial data seamlessly with other types of data stored in the database. For example, you could use PostgreSQL and PostGIS to store and analyze data related to maps, geographical features, addresses, routes, and more.

Some of PostGIS features :

  • 1. Spatial Data Storage: Store different types of spatial data such as points, lines, polygons, and multi-geometries, in both 2D and 3D data.
  • 2. Spatial Indexing: Quickly search and retrieve spatial data based on its location.
  • 3. Spatial Data Storage: Store different types of spatial data such as points, lines, polygons, and multi-geometries, in both 2D and 3D data.
  • 4. Spatial Functions: A wide range of spatial functions that allow you to filter and analyze spatial data, measuring distances and areas, intersecting geometries, buffering, and more.
  • 5. Geometry Processing: Tools for processing and manipulating geometry data, such as simplification, conversion, and generalization.
  • 6. Raster Data Support: Storage and processing of raster data, such as elevation data and weather data.
  • 7. Geocoding and Reverse Geocoding: Functions for geocoding and reverse geocoding.
  • 8. Integration: Access and work with PostGIS using third party tools such as QGIS, GeoServer, MapServer, ArcGIS, Tableau.
